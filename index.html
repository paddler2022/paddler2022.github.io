I am a first-year Master of Engineering student at the Graduate School of Information, Production and Systems, Waseda University, Japan. I previously earned an MSc in Electrical & Electronics Engineering (minor in Computer Science) from EPFL, Switzerland, and a BEng in Electrical Engineering & Automation from the Harbin Institute of Technology, China.

My research sits at the intersection of information retrieval and LLM-based agents. I build benchmarks and diagnostic analyses that expose where current retrievers and deep-research agents break down — particularly in multilingual and cross-lingual settings — and I am increasingly interested in agentic systems for tasks such as remote-sensing image restoration.

  1. IR
    ACL 2026 Findings

    Code-Switching Information Retrieval: Benchmarks, Analysis, and the Limits of Current Retrievers

    Yuheng Lu*, …co-authors…

    Two code-switching retrieval benchmarks — CSR-L (human-annotated, En–Zh / En–Ja) and CS-MTEB (11 tasks, 9 language pairs). Benchmarking four retriever families reveals code-switching degradation of up to 27%; a PCA analysis shows the failure is representational, and a lexicon-based vocabulary expansion partially closes the gap.

  2. DR
    Preprint

    Beyond Monolingual Deep Research: Evaluating Agents and Retrievers with Cross-Lingual BrowseComp-Plus

    Yuheng Lu*, …co-authors…

    XBCP — the first benchmark for cross-lingual deep research — extends BrowseComp-Plus into cross-lingual and multilingual settings across 12 languages. Evaluating 4 deep-research agents × 5 retrievers reveals 16–23 pp accuracy drops with translated evidence and, via oracle retrieval, isolates a persistent agent-side integration bottleneck beyond retrieval failure.

    arXiv Code
  3. SR
    Under Review

    Social-BRIGHT: Benchmarking Socially Grounded Reasoning in Information Retrieval

    …co-authors…, Yuheng Lu, …

    A benchmark casting four forms of social interpretation into full-corpus retrieval (2,154 queries with matched hard-negative pairs). A matched-pair accuracy (MPA) metric isolates social discrimination from recall; evaluating 18+ retrievers shows the bottleneck is fine-grained discrimination, and three enhancements yield only selective gains.

    arXiv & code coming soon
  4. RS
    Ongoing

    Agentic Orchestration of Single-Degradation Experts for Compound Remote-Sensing Restoration

    …co-authors…, Yuheng Lu, …

    An LLM-orchestrated agent that decomposes compound degradations and composes single-degradation expert tools step by step, outperforming all-in-one models. A rollback-guarded loop keeps only quality-improving steps (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS) for monotonic improvement, backed by a self-maintaining tool library with automatic discovery and verification-based retirement.

    arXiv & code coming soon
